Car Parking game

3D vehicle modeling, textures, mapping, scenery and 2D game interface.

So, picture me sitting at my design station, fueled by creativity and armed with my go-to tools—Blender and Photoshop. Blender, my digital playground for 3D modeling and animation, is where the magic begins. I use it to sculpt the very fabric of our game environment—building towering structures, carving out intricate roads, and defining parking spaces. The flexibility of Blender allows me to breathe life into my imagination, ensuring every element is not just created but crafted for an immersive gaming experience.

When it comes to vehicles, Blender is my co-pilot once again. I shape and mold our digital cars with precision, paying attention to every curve and detail. As they come to life, Blender’s animation features give them that realistic touch—steering, accelerating, and braking in a way that feels not just scripted but dynamic and engaging for our players.

Enter Photoshop, my trusted companion in the realm of textures and materials. I turn to Photoshop to design and refine texture maps, giving surfaces that extra oomph. Asphalt for the roads, concrete for the parking spaces—Photoshop lets me add those finishing touches that elevate the visual appeal of our game. It’s like putting the final brushstrokes on a digital canvas.

Now, let’s talk user interface design. Photoshop steps into the spotlight once more as I create buttons, menus, and HUD elements. It’s not just about functionality; it’s about ensuring a seamless integration of the user interface with the overall aesthetics of the game. Photoshop’s fine-tuning capabilities come in handy, making sure our interface is not only user-friendly but also a visual delight.

As I navigate through the animation phase, Blender and Photoshop become my dynamic duo. Blender breathes life into our creations, and Photoshop lets me tweak and refine animations. It’s a collaborative process, ensuring smooth transitions and a polished final product.

Testing time arrives, and Blender helps me identify any hiccups in the 3D models or animations. Meanwhile, Photoshop becomes my optimization toolkit, allowing me to refine textures and visuals for optimal performance across various devices.

In this digital dance of creativity and technology, Blender and Photoshop aren’t just tools—they’re extensions of my artistic vision. Together, they help me shape a 3D car parking game that’s not just a project but a personal journey of design and innovation.
